AI in Dental Caries Diagnostics

About this study

  • This study investigates how dental professionals interact and view different interface displays of AI-based decision support systems when diagnosing proximal caries from bite-wing radiographs.
  • A series of dental radiographs will be presented, with an AI assistant providing relevant diagnostic recommendations, under 3 interface conditions.
  • The AI was trained on a dataset of bitewing radiographs, and has achieved a diagnostic accuracy of 90%.
  • Your responses will help us design AI tools that are reliable and more effective in dental settings.

Task: Involves reviewing 12 bite-wing radiographs across 3 different AI interface types and confirm the presence or absence of proximal caries.

A questionnaire focused on the user experience with the AI interface will be administered after completing each interface condition.

Duration: Approximately 15–20 minutes.

Eligibility: Licensed dental professionals.

Study Debrief

About this study

Please read the following information carefully.

True purpose of this study:

This study was designed to investigate over-reliance on AI, in the context of dental diagnostics. The aim was to understand how different interface designs can influence the extent to which dental professionals rely on AI recommendations, and in turn affect their diagnostic accuracy.

Why the full purpose was not disclosed in advance:

Informing participants of the true aim before participation would have influenced their behaviour in ways that could invalidate the results. For this reason, the introduction described the study more broadly as an evaluation of an AI-assisted diagnosis, without specifying the over-reliance hypothesis.

Your responses will be analysed alongside those of other participants to determine how interface design influences AI over-reliance. No individual responses will be reported, only aggregated & anonymised data.

Now that you are aware of the study's true purpose, you have the right to withdraw your data if you do not wish it to be used. To do so, please contact using the details given below within two weeks of completing the study.
