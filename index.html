Research Study

Dental AI Diagnostic Study

About this study

This study investigates how dental professionals interact with AI-based decision support systems when diagnosing proximal caries from bite-wing radiographs. Your responses will help us design AI tools that are safer and more effective in clinical settings.

Task: Review radiographs and provide diagnoses across three different AI interface types.

Duration: Approximately 15–20 minutes.

Eligibility: Qualified dental professionals only.

Data: Collected anonymously for research purposes.

Study Debrief

About this study

Please read the following information carefully.

True purpose of this study

This study was designed to investigate over-reliance on AI — specifically, how the way in which an AI recommendation is presented (immediately, after an initial decision, or on request) affects the diagnostic accuracy of dental professionals.

Why the full purpose was not disclosed in advance

Informing participants of the true aim before participation would have changed behaviour in ways that invalidate the results — a phenomenon known as demand characteristics. For this reason, the introduction described the study more broadly as an evaluation of AI-assisted diagnosis, without specifying the over-reliance hypothesis.

This use of partial concealment is a recognised and ethically approved methodology in behavioural and human factors research. The study protocol, including this deceptive element, was reviewed and approved prior to data collection.

Your rights

Now that you are aware of the study's true purpose, you have the right to withdraw your data if you do not wish it to be used. To do so, please contact the principal investigator using the details below within two weeks of completing the study. If we do not hear from you, we will assume you are content for your anonymised data to be included.

What happens next

Your responses will be analysed alongside those of other participants to determine how interface design influences AI over-reliance. Findings will be written up for academic publication. No individual responses will be reported — only aggregated, anonymised data.
